Project Details

Radio Systems for IoT Devices

Description

This project focuses on developing and analyzing radio systems for IoT devices, with an emphasis on filter modeling for Software-Defined Radio (SDR) applications. It includes digital signal simulations in both time and frequency domains, integrating Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiplexing (OFDM) techniques. The transmission chain is modeled and simulated to evaluate interference sources and explore performance trade-offs in IoT radio systems.

  • Context: Project for the course "Systèmes radio pour les objets connectés" in the Embedded Systems and Connected Objects master's program.
  • Local: Institut Polytechnique de Grenoble (INP Grenoble), Grenoble, France
  • Duration: 5 months
  • Team Size: 2 people
  • Timeline: Sep 2023 - Jan 2024

Technologies Used

  • Concepts: Software-Defined Radio (SDR), Digital and Analog Filter Modeling, OFDM, Transmission Chain Analysis

Tasks

  • Modeled digital and analog filters applicable.
  • Simulated digital signals in time and frequency domains, incorporating OFDM concepts.
  • Developed and simulated a transmission chain to assess interference sources and performance trade-offs.
  • Analyzed simulation results to optimize filter design and improve IoT communication reliability.

Project Details

Aerodesign Competition Team

Description

Carancho Aerodesign, founded in 2004, is a project at the Federal University of Santa Maria (UFSM) that brings together students from various engineering disciplines to design and construct radio-controlled aircraft. The team participates annually in the SAE Brasil Aerodesign competition, where students showcase their skills in aeronautical engineering. The project aims to develop innovative solutions in aircraft construction, focusing on structural efficiency and aerodynamics.

  • Context: Aerodesign Competition Team.
  • Local: Santa Maria, Brazil
  • Duration: 2 years
  • Team Size: 30 person
  • Timeline: May 2020 - Apr 2022

Technologies Used

  • Programming Language: Python, Matlab, C
  • Tools: SolidWorks, Abaqus, Ansys, Excel, XFLR5
  • Techniques: Loads analysis, aeroelasticity, structural integrity testing

Tasks

  • Led a structural block comprising three departments: Structures Analysis, Loads and Aeroelasticity, and CAD, managing task assignments and progress tracking.
  • Achieved 10th place in the national SAE Aerodesign competition in 2021, demonstrating expertise in analyzing structural integrity and aeroelastic behavior of aircraft.
  • Conducted wing load analysis for structural integrity under various flight conditions.
  • Performed load analysis on vertical tail and control surfaces to ensure stability.
  • Analyzed landing gear loads for multiple landing scenarios and braking conditions.
  • Executed engine mount load analysis, evaluating vertical and lateral forces.

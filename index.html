About Me

Where it Started

Have you ever loved something so deeply that you couldn't even explain why?

Well… that's exactly how I feel about aerospace engineering.

I grew up in Brazil, in a challenging socioeconomic environment, living in peripheral areas where safety wasn't always a given. In a place where dreams sometimes felt limited by reality, I learned to find joy in whatever I had at home. Somehow, amidst the daily struggles, my fascination with aerospace engineering took root. Watching planes soar above, defying gravity and expectations, sparked something in me—a desire to explore, to understand, and to one day be part of that world.

2019

High School

During high school, resources were often limited, and there were challenges with teacher availability, infrastructure, and financial support.

With the help of some teachers who provided me with educational materials, as well as the support of my family and friends, I studied on my own at home during all my free time to prepare for the highly competitive entrance exams for aerospace engineering college.

2020

Aerospace Engineering

Getting accepted into the second most competitive degree program in Brazil was a big challenge, but my difficulties didn’t end there; in fact, they got even tougher over the years.

Being in an elite environment without coming from an elite background is a big challenge. My first few years were spent trying to catch up on the technical knowledge I lacked due to my basic educational background. At the same time, I had to face the challenges posed by the pandemic that had just begun.

Over the years, as I caught up to the technical level of my classmates, I also tried to bring people from similar backgrounds into these spaces. I founded and participated in several projects aimed at scientific outreach.

2023

Double Degree in Embedded Systems

Thanks to my outstanding academic performance, I earned a government scholarship from Brazil to study in France for two years.

The adaptation process in France was quite challenging, especially due to the language barriers and the shift in specialization from aerospace engineering to embedded systems. Now that I have gained a solid understanding of embedded systems, I truly believe I am ready to launch my professional career.
